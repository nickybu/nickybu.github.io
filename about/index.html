I grew up in Malta, a pretty small island (316 km2) in the middle of the Mediterranean. My childhood and teenage years were spent attending St. Edward’s College, through which I received an International Baccalaureate diploma in 2015. I then completed a BSc in Software Development from The University of Malta. During this three year period, I gained work experience through two internships at Ricston Ltd and Betsson Group, as a MuleSoft developer and full-stack web developer respectively.

Wishing to experience life beyond the small rock, I decided to move to Manchester to read for an MSc in Advanced Computer Science at The University of Manchester specialising in AI. This presented me with opportunities to explore interests outside of my course; mainly in physics and astronomy. I carried out my dissertation under the supervision of Prof. Steve Furber and Dr. Oliver Rhodes, both involved in the SpiNNaker project. This led to me learning about neuromorphic computing, spiking neural networks and spatial navigation mechanisms in the mammalian brain. Subsequently, I relocated to Cambridge to join BIOS as a graduate ML engineer, where I worked on peripheral nerve recording simulation.

Besides computer science-related interests, I appreciate good UI/UX design and find myself becoming increasingly interested in startups and animation. On a Saturday evening I could be found enjoying a pint of beer 🍺 at a live gig 🎸 or stand up set 🎤. Music, podcasts, reading and comedy are a few hobbies of mine.
